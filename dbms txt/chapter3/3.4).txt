Primary keys are unique identifiers for records in a database table. They serve as a means to uniquely identify each record, ensuring that no two records in the table have the same primary key. Below are suggested primary keys for the scenarios mentioned earlier:

Employee Information:
Primary Key: EmployeeID
Product Information:
Primary Key: ProductCode
Customer Information:
Primary Key: CustomerID
Purchase Information:
Primary Key: TransactionID
Supplier Information:
Primary Key: SupplierID
Order Information:
Primary Key: OrderID
Inventory Information:
Primary Key: Combination of ProductCode and WarehouseID
Employee Schedule:
Primary Key: Combination of EmployeeID and Date
Supplier Catalog:
Primary Key: Combination of SupplierID and ProductCode
Customer Orders:
Primary Key: Combination of CustomerID, ProductCode, and OrderDate
